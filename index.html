User Research · May 2026
Not an idea.
A validated observation.

Before a single screen was designed, I interviewed two real Ajo participants to understand what actually matters — and what would kill adoption of a digital version. Every design decision in this pitch maps back to what they said.

Finding 01
Trust is personal — always
Both respondents built groups only through personal networks. No stranger would be accepted regardless of any app verification. MonieCircle is private-group only — closed circles of people who already know each other. By design, not compromise.

"You are the one bringing them in. If they misbehave, it is on you."

— Respondent 1, Active Ajo Coordinator
Finding 02
The coordinator carries every burden
Reminders, disputes, covering shortfalls — all on one person, every single cycle. MonieCircle automates exactly these tasks: auto-deduction, contribution tracking, and payout disbursement. The coordinator becomes an organiser, not a debt collector.

"You have to remind them — this is the due date — enforcing the boundaries. That is the most stressful part."

— Respondent 1
Finding 03
Delayed payouts are a recurring, costly pain
Two delayed payouts experienced by Respondent 2 — both because the coordinator personally bridged shortfalls while waiting on late contributors. Auto-deduction on due date plus Monie Sense early nudges plus Moniepoint's 1M+ POS terminal network for cash-in removes every excuse for lateness.
Finding 04
The app must organise — not hold money
Both respondents were uncomfortable with any app "holding" their funds. They want a facilitation layer — not a financial custodian. MonieCircle is positioned as "your Ajo, organised" — not a savings product. Moniepoint facilitates. The trust stays human.

"The app verifying them does not mean they will not withdraw and disappear."

— Respondent 2
Finding 05
Fees must be group-agreed — never imposed
Respondent 1 rejected any platform fee outright. Respondent 2 accepted it only if the group collectively agrees before the cycle begins. MonieCircle's fee model is transparent and group-agreed at setup. Moniepoint never deducts silently.
Finding 06
A trust safety net would close the deal
Respondent 2 said a traceability mechanism or payout guarantee would significantly increase their confidence in using a digital version. This directly validates MonieSense's Ajo Trust Score — a behavioural reliability rating shown before anyone joins a circle.
The verdict:
Real problem. Conditional appetite.
Adoption will happen if the app feels like a trusted organiser for known groups — not a platform that introduces financial risk. MonieCircle was designed precisely around these conditions, one finding at a time.
✅ Private groups only — closed circles, no strangers by design
✅ App as organiser — not a bank holding or controlling money
✅ Coordinator-first — automates their most painful and stressful tasks
✅ Group-agreed fees — no platform surprises, ever
✅ Trust Score safety net — behavioural reliability before joining any circle

01 · HeroMonieCircle — "Ajo, organised"
Your community, your turn+

What it is

A transparent, in-app rotating savings group built for private circles of trusted people. Admin creates the group, adds members by phone or account number only, sets the contribution amount, payout rotation, and monthly schedule. Every member sees the live circle balance on their own device. The admin has no direct access to pooled contributions and cannot hide a kobo.

Research-backed: Both respondents confirmed they only join groups with people they personally know. Private-group only — by design.

What competitors do vs what we do better

Bankly Group Contributions offers digital Ajo at 15% interest — but it is a standalone microfinance app with limited reach and no AI layer.

OneBank (Sterling) has a basic Ajo feature — but has neither Moniepoint's 16M user base, 1M+ POS network, nor any intelligence layer.

MonieCircle inside Moniepoint means every member already has a bank account. Auto-deduction works instantly. Cash-in is possible via 1 million POS terminals across all 774 local governments. No other Ajo product in Nigeria can say that.

The moat

Millions of informal Ajo groups move onto a regulated, transparent platform. Every new member is a potential Moniepoint personal banking user — recruited organically by their own trusted circle. The Ajo group becomes a Moniepoint acquisition engine.

No standalone Ajo app can replicate Moniepoint's infrastructure advantage. This is the first feature that turns a cultural behaviour into a banking flywheel.

First major bank to own Ajo at this scale
02MonieGoal — Shared Savings, Smarter
Same dream, shared hustle+

What it is

Two or more people set a shared savings goal — a car, rent, a business, a trip. Each contributes to one pooled target. All parties see the real-time breakdown of who contributed what, how much is in the pot, and exactly how close they are to the goal. Interest accrues on the pooled balance. Nobody can withdraw without a process.

Honest context: Piggyvest has Group Target Savings and Cowrywise has Money Duo. MonieGoal goes further — here is exactly how and why.

What competitors do vs what we do better

Piggyvest Group Target Savings — each member's funds stay completely separate. There is no shared pot. No mutual accountability on a single balance. No approval layer. It is parallel individual savings toward a common target, not true joint savings.

Cowrywise Money Duo — limited to exactly two people, locked to one year, and exists on a standalone savings app with no banking ecosystem around it.

MonieGoal is multi-party (two or more), flexible on timeline, lives inside a full banking product — and has an AI pace intelligence layer no competitor offers. It is the only true shared savings pot in Nigerian fintech.

The moat

Couples, business partners, friend groups, and families currently manage shared goals across WhatsApp threads, spreadsheets, and informal trust. MonieGoal gives them a single accountable pot inside a bank they already use.

The key differentiator from every competitor is Moniepoint's banking backbone — transfers, auto-contributions, and interest all work natively. No other group savings product in Nigeria has this.

True shared pot — no equivalent in Nigerian banking
03MonieVault — Joint Control
Shared money, shared trust+

What it is

A shared wallet created within a personal Moniepoint account where any withdrawal requires approval from all parties before money moves. Interest-bearing. Multi-party. Approval gates built in by default. Designed for couples, co-founders, siblings, and anyone who wants to save together with full mutual accountability.

What competitors do vs what we do better

Traditional joint bank accounts require physical presence, marriage certificates, and paperwork. They have no approval flow — either party can withdraw without permission.

No Nigerian fintech has a digital multi-party approval-gated wallet with interest. MonieVault is the first. The closest global comparison is a trust account — but MonieVault is digital, instant, and built for everyday Nigerians who do not have access to wealth management products.

The moat

Solves a deeply human problem — saving together without fighting about spending. The approval gate is the entire product insight: it forces conscious, mutual decision-making before any money moves. Interest rewards the discipline of keeping it locked.

Zero competition in Nigerian fintech. This is genuinely unbuilt territory.

Completely uncharted in Nigerian fintech
04MoniePocket — Parent to Child
Send love, schedule it+

What it is

A parent-controlled wallet linked to a child's Moniepoint account. Parents fund it, set automatic allowance schedules, and approve or decline every withdrawal request. The child must give a reason before any money moves. Parents receive a weekly AI-generated spend digest showing exactly where the money went and suggesting smarter scheduling.

What competitors do vs what we do better

Globally, Greenlight (USA) and GoHenry (UK) have built parent-child finance tools — but neither exists in Nigeria.

Google Pay India launched Pocket Money via UPI Circle in December 2025 — but only in India, and without the AI digest layer.

In Nigeria, zero. No bank, no fintech, no app has built this. MoniePocket is Nigeria's first. And unlike global competitors, it sits inside a full banking product — meaning parents and children are already on the same platform.

The moat

Captures an entirely new user segment — students, secondary school boarders, university students, and their busy parents. Every Nigerian parent who has ever forgotten to send allowance or argued with a child about money is the target user.

The AI digest transforms MoniePocket from a transfer tool into a parenting intelligence product — and nothing like it exists anywhere in African fintech today.

Nigeria's first family finance feature
MonieCircle · User Flow
How it works,
step by step.

Every step designed around a validated user insight. No new app. No extra signup. Just open Moniepoint.

Step 01
Create Circle
Admin creates a private group, sets name, contribution amount, start date, and payout rotation. Members invited by phone or account number only — nobody joins without a known connection.
Research-backed: groups built from personal networks only.
Step 02
Members Accept
Each member receives a notification and reviews the full terms — amount, their payout position, schedule — before confirming. Nobody joins blind or uninformed.
Step 03 · Key
Full Transparency
Every member sees a live dashboard — who paid, who has not, the circle balance, and next payout date. The admin cannot hide anything from anyone. This is the feature that kills fraud.
Research-backed: trust requires complete visibility.
Step 04 · Key
Auto-Deduction
Moniepoint auto-debits each member on the due date. MonieSense nudges low-balance members 3 days early. Cash-in is available via any of Moniepoint's 1M+ POS terminals nationwide.
Research-backed: delayed payouts are the number one coordinator pain point.
Step 05
Payout Day
Funds minus the group-agreed platform fee auto-transfer to that month's recipient on schedule. Zero manual action. Zero coordinator stress. Zero delay.
Step 06
Cycle Repeats
Circle auto-resets for the next recipient. Full history is permanently logged and viewable by all members. Circle renews or dissolves after the final payout.
The Intelligence Layer
MonieSense —
what Revolut has.
What Nigeria does not yet.

On March 20, 2026, Starling launched the UK's first agentic AI banking assistant using Google Gemini. On April 9, 2026, Revolut launched AIR to 13 million UK customers. Both built for the UK. Nothing like this exists for Nigeria. MonieSense is that layer — built for our languages, our behaviours, and our realities. And every feature below maps directly to a validated pain point from our research.

For MonieCircle · Research-backed
Default Prediction
AI monitors each member's income and spending patterns. If a balance drops dangerously close to the contribution due date, MonieSense nudges them privately — before the group notices, before it becomes drama. Directly addresses the number one coordinator pain point from our interviews.
For MonieCircle · Research-backed
Ajo Trust Score
A behavioural reliability rating built from each member's Moniepoint transaction history — income consistency, savings behaviour, bill payment patterns. Shown before anyone joins a circle. No credit bureau needed. Directly answers the traceability need from our interviews. No Ajo app in Nigeria offers this.
For MonieGoal
Goal Pace Intelligence
AI tracks group contribution pace in real time and tells members proactively: "At this rate, you will hit your goal 3 weeks late — here is how to close the gap." Accountability without the awkward conversation. Neither Piggyvest nor Cowrywise offers this.
For MoniePocket
Parent Intelligence Digest
AI learns the child's spending patterns over time. Parents receive a weekly digest: "Your child tends to run low mid-week. Consider scheduling ₦5,000 on Wednesdays." Parenting, powered by data. No equivalent exists in any Nigerian fintech product.
Across All Features
Conversational Money Assistant
Type or voice-note in Pidgin, Yoruba, Igbo, or English: "How far, how much I don spend on food this month?" MonieSense answers instantly from your transaction data. Nigeria's first truly local AI money assistant — no menus, just conversation.
Across All Features
Financial Health Score
Weekly personalised score built from income, spending, and savings patterns. Not generic advice — your data, your score, your specific next step. Built with the same philosophy as what Revolut's AIR and Starling's Assistant are delivering in the UK. Delivered for Nigeria.
